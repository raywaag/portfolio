Designing for Development and Developing for Design

An underrated role in UX is the designer-developer handoff. Designs could be hindered by little understanding on the development team, workflows can take a hit and the lack of communication can hinder the success of the project.

For one, designers should work alongside the developers to answer questions and offer guidance where needed. Some say they should work in the same building on the same floor and if you want to really go big, at the same desk. In large companies this may not be feasible but luckily there are ways to communicate beyond face to face like video calls or team chat environments. Always communicate. Always.

Written deliverables. This is something I have found very helpful for me, for developers and for business analysts who are tasked to write out project requirements and user stories. Developers may not be equipped with proper UX knowledge, which is fine - they didn’t go to school for that. BUT written modifications or key components will help the developer understand their significance in the application and why they are necessary. In app audits or re-skins, this deliverable helps close-in on the main areas of modification. Tools. There are many zooms and growing on the market to ease the design to developer gap. At HCA, we use InVision and its Inspect tool. As a designer and developer I have been able to practice on both sides of the board. I can speak highly of InVision Inspect tool as it really helps measuring and defining components on a screen. With our design system, Neutron, this bridge between design and development is improving as we can develop our the components in the system and create our own framework. With this, I’ve been able to develop a complex screen in a day’s time.

With designers and developers, there is no hierarchy and you’re on the same team. Build a relationship between the two teams that allows for support throughout the development process of the application. This increases the product’s success and builds new friendships.
