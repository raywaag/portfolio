What is Skeet Shot?

A collection of a southern family's distillery, Skeet Shot Distillery. This site is a residence of their crafts, recipes, family biographies and communication.

When given the task of designing the internet-interior of the Distillery's home, it was clear that the atmosphere should convey family, collectiveness and, of course, the original Skeet.

My Roles

UX Designer Information Architect Client Consultant Interaction Designer

Research

Being unfamiliar with distilleries in general to creating a site accommodating to all users, I heavily researched current distillery sites such as Jack Daniel's and the locals like Glacier Distillery. Using the current market as a template, I was able to create a sitemap to successfully portray the Skeet Shot family, business and friendliness.

I found the basic milestones of distillery sites to be a hierarchy. The utmost being the general type of liquor to the drink itself and specifics. It was also important to include drink recipe ideas and, in this case, the history of Skeet Shot Distillery and the family behind it.

Above are current market distillery sites that helped me conjure Skeet Shot's.

Detailing the Research

After analyzing the market sites it was vital to include the following in Skeet Shot's site.

  • The Story. This is important. There are many greats choices of liquor out there so the story behind Skeet Shot really sells it to audience personas not in the know.
  • Imagery. Not only of the alcohol but the family as well. As a photographer, I really believe in the power of quality imagery. In this tense, the images help create a visual friendship with the Skeet family as well as see their property and the beautiful environment in which the drinks are made.
  • Recipes. As seen on many sites, recipes of their drinks and mixology ideas are infamous. Providing these mixologies gives visitors some ideas on ways to drink that are not just straight.
  • Drink details. The copywriting and details here were a great learning opportunity. I don't drink liquor, I admit it. So I was pretty lost on how to convey a drink to an audience that are looking for specifics. These details included Proof, ABV, smoothness and seasonality of the drinks.
  • Product page. This goes with little saying, but product pages are essential to every online store. An overview of the products with proper filtering give the user the control they require.
  • Are you 21 or older? Every single bar, brewery, Distillery and wine site I visited prompted me with a message:

    Are you 21 or older?

    This was a simple add. Some message were more annoying than others. As a user, this experience matters. It is the first impression of any alcohol site. The key things here were not having the user input a date (time-consuming), order the buttons properly (primary on right , secondary on left) and stating a clear "Yes, I'm older" or "No I am not older" message.
