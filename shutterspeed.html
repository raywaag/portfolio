About


Shutterspeed efficiently connects clients and photographers within location, price range and photographic genre. With Shutterspeed, photographers can profile themselves, showcase their work, present testimonials and connect with clients. Similarly, clients can post jobs, search and find photographers within their area and budget as well as have profiles and contact photographers.


Freelance photographers, like many small business entrepreneurs, face the obstacles of promotion and advertising while also developing a brand for themselves, connecting with their clients efficiently and doing so without fraudulent data. Photographers often have to rely on third-party applications such as Facebook or Instagram to connect with potential clients and showcase their work. This gives an unfair advantage to the more “popular” photographers, leaving other very skilled photographers in the dark. Shutterspeed treats everyone equally and is not based on ratings or popularity. Clients also may have a difficult time finding a photographer that suits them and their specs especially if they are not social media users. Word of mouth and a good SEO website is all they have in their toolbox.


Clients in need of photographers who are seeking to search with ease, security and efficiency within a budget and location. Likewise, growing photographers promoting their business, seeking clientele and have the desire to do so with security and assurance.

The Challenge

  • Accomodating a dual audience (photographers and general public clients) with a large age spectrum.
  • As ShutterSpeed is a website, it is to be designed for desktop, tablet and mobile devices.
  • The team is catalyzed by photographers and members with client experience, giving us the upperhand of proper knowledge to know how a photographer reads information and how a potential client searches for their needs. This, however, does not mean we were to neglect the phase of user testing.
  • ShutterSpeed combines many features like job boards, filtration, messaging, profiles, ratings and live reviews. This means UX research of multiple platforms and full-stack development.

Research

ShutterSpeed's market competitors include Shootster, Care.com and SnapShot. Think aloud Protocals were executed during hierarchical task analyses. Through this research we found key ways to decrease error rates and time on task and increase enjoyment and retention rates. Exhibiting more graphical content and less verbaige with simple, clear pathways executing immediate user feedback were a prioritizations needed in the designer phase of the site. Other fixes include proper functionality (accurate code maps), large mobile icons and detailed descriptions to avoid misinformation.

Key Pain Points

  • Outdated site information and users. A big issue with related sites are their seeming ability to stay up to date. A client reaching out to an inactive photographer will not service anyone. ShutterSpeed will phase out accounts upon their inactivity. Unlike Care.com and Shootster, some site designs are very outdated as well. Photography is a technical profession and if there is not a corresponding technical site, users may be led to believe that ShutterSpeed has a lack of mastery and skill in teh field and therefore are not credible. You shouldn't judge a book by its cover, yet everyone does.
  • Too much verbaige. It is possible for a site to be very word-heavy. Just like a magazine page or social media post, visuals with too many words are often overlooked. Especially as a photography site, our mission for ShutterSpeed was to create an environment with congregating colors and esteemed images.
  • Catering to the wrong audience. As said before, ShutterSpeed is geared towards both photographers and clients. Some sites have a landing page that say "Need a photographer?" in large text and, of course, a jumbotron. But waht about the clients? This lead us to base our design more on Care.com's landing page that greets the user with options like a fork in the road...Photographers go this way, clients go with way.
  • Photographer-specific. Besides the key factors that would make ShutterSpeed successful by design, there are not many freelance-friendly sites that cater specifically to photographers. This gives us a good gap in the market to comfortably reach victory.

Wireframes

Ease, simplicity and detail were three concepts in mind when the wireframes were designed.


Images. Images lead a great deal of our design methods. Gallery sections, photographer highlights and features photographs were confident decisions while scheming ShutterSpeed's format. Another must-have was the as talked about "fork in the road" on the landing page. This gives the user an immediate option to distinguish themselves and recieve the proper content they need.

UX Fundamentals

    A hierarchical task analysis was performed on competitor, Care.com, to better understand the alterations that needed to be made or features to include in order for ShutterSpeed to reach its utmost success.

  • Time on task: In user testing, Care.com's amount of questions when it came to hiring a caretaker was very time-consuming. On top of that, the site feedback was inaccurate, leaving the user misinformed and misdirected. The user would have benefited more from a questionnaire with less verbaige, a greater opportunity to "choose all" and more accurate feedback.
  • Error Rate: This lead us to another finding of errors. Care.com had errors with button feedback which wasted time and frustrated the user. To avoid errors, ShutterSpeed's development would need to be detailed, accurate and user-friendly.
  • Retention Rate: Care.com did well at letting the user know where they were and how they got there. Our plan with ShutterSpeed and it's filtering capablitiy upon searches (adding and deleting filters as well) would give us the means of reminding the user of where they are and why they are there.
  • User Enjoyment: Care.com's use of color, size and wording give the user experience praise. Simple. Apparent. Alluring. Those design qualities make the user want to keep exploring deeper into the site.

Design

ShutterSpeed's color palette was influenced by both Shootster and Care.com to encompass the neighborly feel with professionalism of a workplace. The interface is simple and clean to fashion minimalism and navigability. The landing page is similar to Shootster's while the split road in the upper section of the home is similar to that of Care.com.






Site Walkthrough

As you enter ShutterSpeed, the option to search a photographer or genre is presented to you or, if you are a photographer, you may log in / sign up.

Let's say you are a photographer and you log in, you are then taken to your profile and the navigation bar is altered to display settings, messages and log out icons. From here, you can view any unread messages from clients or other photographers, read reviews people have written for you or explore the feature, home and back-office pages of the site.

As a client, you can search for your photographer for instance under the "wedding" genre and you'll be presented with results. These results can then be filtered by location, budget and genre as well.

By scrolling through the results, you can find the photographer that best suits your needs, view their profile and send a message their way.

Just visiting? No problem! Explore ShutterSpeed's feature photographs, photographer options, job listing and more!
