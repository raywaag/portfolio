Design Systems

A design system is an established UI library containing components big and small dedicated to cohesive, accessible, efficient and effective interfaces across all products. At HCA Healthcare, we implemented atomic design. Ever heard of Brad Frost? He is the creator of this atomic methodology. Let’s take a look. The atomic design format offers compartmentalizing for all UI components in the library. The hierarchy is as follows...

Atoms

Atoms are the core elements that further can be combined to make more complex components. These consist of things like buttons, input fields and containers.

Molecules

These are a combination of atoms to create a bit more complex components like form fields with labels, Tabs and table rows.

Organisms

Molecules plus atoms group in ways to create organisms. The are the most complex of the three levels. Organisms are components like Tab Groups, Login Forms and Full Search bars.

Templates

There are templates and pages. For the purpose of our corporate-wide design system, we established a set of templates to be used in product design and front-end development with our design system. These templates are very generic and can be customized in any way to suite a specific project. Essentially, they are boilerplates to help teams find a starting point. Templates, for instance, include login pages, 404 states, dashboards and forms.

I have had the opportunity to be involved in the design system’s design growth and the development of the UI code library. This has really helped ensure that the code library adheres to the design guidelines and is as available as possible given that these components are being used in numerous ways.

I have found this to be a challenge in some areas and an incredible learning opportunity in others. By beta testing new platform kits like our iOS design system and gathering feedback from front-end developers, our design system has made a company of over 200,000 employees one big community.
