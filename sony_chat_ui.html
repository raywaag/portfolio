SONY Live Chat UI

Enhance the experience of communication.

About

We've all encountered an either annoying or obliging live chat box while visiting a site. Maybe we didn't need help but the pop-up was revealed anyways. Maybe we did need help but the chat options were not extensive enough. Maybe the chat was SO detailed, that we were overwhelmed and we opted out of live chat help.


These are all issues that were confronted in the design on this SONY chat. It was important that the features were perectly amounted to avoid user frustration or engulfing them with chat features.


Upon research and experience, user testing revealed that some prominent features were like/disliking the operator, email ability to resort a step beyond live chat and send an emil to the company, adding images incase proof of evidence or a more detailed descripton of the product is desired and images of the company team member. When a user sees who they are chatting with, it allows for a less threatened experience.

The Challenge

  • Mobile and Desktop chat to be designed.
  • Research other help chats to capture useful and impractical features.
  • A customer in need of help is highly likely in distress, the interface needs to manifest a comfortable environment for the user.
  • Match SONY's dark theme but not to the point of camoflauge.

Design & Prototype

The palette used for this UI harnessed the technological appeal of dark greys and blues but, as stated before, the interface needed to occupy a complimentary color. We went with white to create that distinction between the webpage and the chat box.





User Flow

The user flow, while seeming meager, is important to establish the specs of the interaction.
Upon clicking the bottom chat bar, the box will ease up at a 0.5s rate. This rate became a standard and was also used for the ease out, the mobile interaction and the keybord implementation on the mobile.
