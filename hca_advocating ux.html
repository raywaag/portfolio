Advocating for UX

If you have visited any other sections here you’ve probably heard me say how passionate I am of advocating usability and accessibility. Educating my colleague community of UX significance is a necessity. When I handoff deliverables to a team I expect to not only improve the application at hand. it also future projects or features that the team may encounter. Brief UX Lift summaries or consult/engagement sessions can improve this knowledge handoff. Consistent and reliable communication between the designers and the team also help inform others of proper UX principles.

Another realm that is in dire need of UX education is one I have been in. I have lived in projects where the goal was to get it working. The application’s appeal or usability was an after thought. Or yet, it wasn’t even a thought. Before I studied human factors, as a developer, I never really considered the user. I thought that if all of its functionalities worked correctly, it was great. Alas, UX. I remember as a human factors student I assisted on studies with PTSD veterans and VR systems in effort to heal them of their past traumas. I analyzed the human-computer interaction of extremely shy kids and an iPad application that aimed to treat there isolation. I saw technology change people’s lives.

It wasn’t until then that I realized the technology means nothing without a successful interaction with its human users. I delved into UCD, HCI and UX. Acronyms galore.

Now I am in a position where I can create a critical bond between the developers and their users. UX in the dev world is a bit of a bough but to crack. But I have found that speaking their language and providing summarized research can go a long way. Developers will always care more about whether an application functions properly over its usability. And that’s their job. But it is our job as the UX specialists to steer developers in the direction of functionality and usability.

As the Beatles said,

It’s a long and winding road.

I am pretty sure they were talking about UX and development here.
