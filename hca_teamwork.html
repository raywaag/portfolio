Teamwork Makes the Dream Work

UX specialties are important. Teamwork really does make the UX dream work. Today I’ll go into a bit about the different specialties, how they play a part in the UX process and my role within the process.

UX Analysts & Accessibility SMEs

I am putting this specialty first because they play key roles before the UX process even begins. Especially in the design and development of the HCA design system, Neutron, these team members gather important accessibility standards and HIG guidelines as well as audit post-production applications. HCA is a healthcare company. Inaccessibile applications can lead to lawsuits and unusable products.

These roles analyze user interface guidelines for different platforms like iOS, Responsive Web and 10-foot views. Additionally, they ensure that product design, colors, workflows and code meet the requirements of their users.

UX Researcher

In discovery phases towards design system components, user interviews, prototype testing, persona defining and laying ground for UX methodologies, UX Researchers are extremely important in a product’s success. At HCA Healthcare, we have an amazing researcher dedicated to helping us establish our core design system as we roll out iOS and tvOS kits. Additionally, on a project basis, there are set personas for specific user groups. With these character definitions, UX Designers then have a better idea towards who they are designing for.

This prerequisites the intricate process to then interview current users (if it’s an existing product) or examine current tools on the market and how the product at hand can meet the standards and we can design a familiar, effective application.

Once we have a prototype worthy of testing, the researcher can then go into the field and gather user feedback. This is the beginning of the iterative process of perfecting the application design to user satisfaction and handing it off to the team of developers.

As you can see, UX Researchers work at the core of the user’s experience. They basically put the U in UX.

UX Researcher

In some project, this role also falls under the UX Designer. In others where there are more designers, there can be a designated role for configuring a product’s workflow and architecture. In products with multiple potential roadmaps, features and directions, it is important to focus on how the user will get from point A to point B.

How will the user complete their task?

In some scenarios, the researcher can then test the workflow wireframes with a set of users to get feedback not biased by design or appeal. IA is one of my favorite aspects of UX. It asks for logic and strategy to create a workflow out of an idea.
