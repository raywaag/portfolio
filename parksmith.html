About

ParkSmith is the virtual travel guide to the World's National Parks. It's designed and developed to display interactive data to the user while also emphasizing the nature's beauty.

Discover not only incredible parks, but also the best local eats, places to board, traveler images, the greatest hikes as well as contact forms to tell get in touch with ParkSmith!

Also, stay informed of National Park Service news and updates!

The Challenge

As an adventurer myself, designing and developing a site dedicated to our parks was a really enjoyable experience. Design-wise, it was important to be aware of what travel users prioritize in their sites. This way, a sitemap could be conjured and developed to lead the user on a path to a fantastic nature trip!

  • Back-end server database organization. Stored within the site database is an image gallery with opportunity to add, delete or update table objects.
  • Information. As a travel guide, ParkSmith is very information heavy. This meant a large portion of development was geared towards research - both user and factual.
  • Imagery. From the start it was clear that ParkSmith needed to be a visual experience. Ray Waag Photography provided the image work seen across the site.
  • APIs. Since ParkSmith was SO data-heavy, APIs were needed to fulfill much needed features such as Google Maps to display to the user the locations of the parks and their amenities.
