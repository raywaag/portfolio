About

Paws With A Purpose serves as a third party networking app to discover organizations that offer veterans service dogs with ease, simplicity and credibility.


The process of searching, discovering and reaching out to a service dog organization can be a complex, broad and uneasy journey. Paws With A Purpose is available to create a friendly, navigable environment for veterans to better understand and find organizations that are most suitable to them and their disabilities.


Paws With a Purpose is for approved veterans seeking their fur-ever companion. Age range varies with a mjority above 35 years.

The Challenge

  • Extensive research of the nation's service dog organizations, their specialties and their selection processes.
  • Knowledge of veteran disabilities, they're relativity, commonality and the best options for those specific disabilities.
  • Multi-platform: iOS and android users.
  • Within the design experience, the veteran's disabilities need to be taken into account such as deafness and blindness.
  • PWAP is a visual experience. Images will be needed and retrieved from different organizations. Ray Waag Photography supplied the images for the PWAP application.

Research

Research was heavily invested in service dog organizations and their specifics to better customize PWAP. This allowed the team to develop pathways specialized to the user's disabilty type. Our biggest market competitor is NAGDU Guide & Service Dog Info. We were able to analyze what aspects of the app they did well and what the user reviews liked / disliked about their app. Other service dog applications were accused of being falsified, leaving us a great gap in the market. A big issue NAGDU Guide & Service Dog Info faced was interface appearance. There was no graphical influence to their sitemap which allowed us a chance enhance our app in that area. Also, PWAP is an app specifically for veterans, therefore, our audience is very select allowing us to focus on catering to them and them only.





Market competitor, NAGDU Guide & Service Dog Info.



Key Pain Points

    Firstly, it is important to note that an app with the capabilities of PWAP has yet to exsist on the market. The research comparisions we made were based on the most similar applications.

  • Navigability was an issue with NAGDU Guide & Service Dog Info mainly because, the outside user is unaware of the app's depth. Discovering that there were only a few pages to the app was frustrating and deceiving.
  • This effected the usability of the app as well given that the information was not absorbed in an efficient fashion due to the user's now maddened state of mind.
  • The current market applications are information-heavy. They are appropriate for user's in search of laws and regualtions regarding service dogs. Then again, you do not need an app for that.
    PWAP is designed for the user with disability-specific data. Generalized information can only take the suer so far. With the catering ability of PWAP, the user can find exactly what they need.

Design

We used yellows and blues to achieve feasibility within a patriotic envirnment. We did not want to overtly use red, white and blue. We found that yellow paired well with the brighter blues and sustained a professional stance. Our primary audience is above the age of 35 years, hence big fonts and icons designed to increase accessibilty and decrease the rate of errors.





User Flow

User profile: Retired veteran, new user, struggling to take the first step in finding a suitable service dog. His goal is to find a service dog organization that best suits his disability as well as gain perspective on others' experiences with that organization.

Capability Needed

  • Account Creation / Sign in: Tabs to sign in or create an account. Applicable forms for each tab. "GO" button to enter app.
  • Landing page: Disability selection options: deafness, blindness, ptsd, other.
  • Organization result page: Service dog organizations list according to the selected disability.
  • Organization profile pages: Each organization in the databse needs a profile with their custom contact info, bio and testimonials.
  • User profile: Each user will have a profile visible to other users. It can contain contact information but its primary use will be to share the user's disablilty in PWAP's supportive, homogeneous community.
  • Message Center: Users can chat with each other to obtain more information and gather opinions on the service dog process and coping with it.
  • About us: PWAP's about us page will explain what we do and why we do it.

Required Capabilities

  • Account Creation / Sign in: User back-end databse to store current accounts and insert new ones.
  • Organizaiton Database: A stock of all service dog orgs in The States.
  • Org Filtration: The organizations will need to be filtered per disabilty accomodations.
  • Review writing: The organizaiton profiles wil have the feature for users to rate and review different orgs in effort to help others understand the good or bad experiences had with that organization.
  • Messaging: Messaging ability between users including blocking and a notification center.






UX Fundamentals

  • Time on task: continuous clicks, scrolls and questions can repel a user, leaving them ineffectively informed and leaving the app without users.
    To achieve an organized, timely application, PWAP consisted of clear and evident icons and buttons with the ability to reach the menu or go "back" at any time.
  • Error Rate: A seemingly obvious but easily overlooked component of the design and develpoment is the rate of errors. It is vital for PWAP users to encounter a user experience wit correct paths, formatting and back-end abiltiy.
    The design needs to promote effortless navigation. So that the user can answer where they are, where they came from and where they need to go next.
  • Retention Rate: this will also allow the user to recognize where they are within the app sitemap. Headers reminding the user where they are will ease their recognition of location in the app. For instance, if they select deafness as their diability, they'll meet a list of potential service dog orangizations with a page header reminding them they selected deafness. Likewise, when they proceed and select an organization, they will be directed to the organization's PWAP "profile" in which the organization they are viewing will be evident.
  • User Enjoyment: a very important fundamental to the app's design is the user's enjoyment. Creating a visually appealing interface that includes infographics and imagery along with principal data will capture the user's amusement and compell them to continue the use of PWAP.

PWAP Walkthrough

Initially, the user is granted the option to choose their specific disability. The home page was designed to ease the decision and clarify the options - including the large icons for PWAP's older audience and colors to separate the actions.

From there, they are directed to a list of accommodating organizations. They can review and select the org they find suitable to their preference.

Once selected, the organization's profile will come into view where the user can gain a better understanding of who they are, their contact information and reviews from other veterans who have experience with the organization.
