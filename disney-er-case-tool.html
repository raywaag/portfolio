Employee Interviews & Feedback

The researcher and I conducted several 1-hour long usability studies observing employees use their current tool and competitor tools. We listened and documented their concerns and needs to feed our efforts to design an intuitive and efficient case tool.

🥲 Pain Points

Many employees we spoke with faced similar hardships when interacting with competitor tools or showing us their extremely over-calculated Excel spreadsheet forms. The main points for me going into the design were:

  • Disorientating Navigation - The additions of tabs throughout case management affords unnecessary complexity.
  • Too Many Fields - Case managers would like to see only what is required to successfully manage the case.
  • Valueless Clicks - The amount of clicks to perform a task is a constant area of concern.
  • Unfamiliar Verbiage - The nomenclature is foreign to the wording case managers are used to.
  • Task Variation - Case managers found that there were too many variations of completing a single task.

😎 Opportunities

It was almost a struggle keeping hold of all the opportunities I had coming out of these interviews. It was energizing and encouraging to carry them through to the design phase. A few of the opportunities were:

  • Streamline the Workflow - Render a linear mental model for case management.
  • Profitable Clicks - Create an environment where every step and/or click is beneficial to the case.
  • Clear Correlation - Make evident which claims are associated to which party and outcome.
  • Efficient Real Estate - Make use of the screen’s space to effectively display information and fields.
  • Desired Features - Case export, auto-save, pre-populated form fields, and Outlook integration.

Market Analysis

Interviewing employees gave us a lot of great insight into how these products work and features of current market competitors. As a team, we had to provide a custom design that was the best at a level that made the return on investment worth it for Disney, rather than partnering with a vendor.

Current Workflow

Watching employees interact with ServiceNow and Quickbase, I was able to map out the flow of 15 (FIFTEEN!!) clicks and numerous tabs and pages it took for employees to do what they needed.

Proposed Workflow

By designing an dynamic, single-page case management system, I reduced the time from case creation to case close down to 5 clicks. This is the power of automation and giving users only what they need through targeting and logic.

Walk through the product components below to learn more about features, interface details, and the requirements that led to them.

Submitting a Case

Before, submitting a case would happen manually and it was separate from the form. Case managers would need to create a case and input details into it as part of their job. In the new experience, intake specialists would input all requests and reports into the same form and this would feed into the case management portal. There would be no more duplicative efforts or miscommunications as everything would live in the same system. Accomodations could event be submitted by the employes themselves!

Case Management

The case management page was where all the action happened. Consider it as the "case file." It had details about the case, notes from interviews, and information about the subject and witnesses. This also where the final decision was submitted and the case was closed.
